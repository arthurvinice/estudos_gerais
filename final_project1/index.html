Ada Lovelace

The world's first computer programmer

She is chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is now considered to be the first algorithm intended for implementation on a machine.

Ada's collaboration with Charles Babbage laid the groundwork for her famous notes, where she described an algorithm for the engine to compute Bernoulli numbers. This is now considered the first computer program.

Ada Lovelace's work was largely forgotten for many years, but she is now recognized as a pioneer of computer science. The programming language Ada was named in her honor.

Early Life

Ada Lovelace was born on December 10, 1815, in London, England. She showed an early aptitude for mathematics, influenced by her mother's insistence on a scientific education.
